## 📖 Overview

**isiXhosa** is one of South Africa's 11 official languages, spoken by over **10 million** native speakers and an additional **11 million** as a second language.  
It is widely recognized for its **click consonants**, rich **morphological structure**, and historical importance — notably as the **native tongue of Nelson Mandela**.

This guide offers:
- ✔️ Bilingual sentence examples
- ✔️ Subject–verb agreement tables
- ✔️ Verb morphology breakdowns
- ✔️ GAI-assisted (Generative AI) tasks for deeper understanding
- ✔️ Insights into affixes, modal markers, and learning tips

> **Target Audience**: Linguistics students, educators, AI researchers, and language enthusiasts  
> *(Note: sg → Singular, pl → Plural)*

## 🧠 Part II: Grammar Breakdown

### 📌 2.1 Subject Prefixes

Subject prefixes are used at the beginning of verbs to indicate the subject (who is doing the action).

| Subject     | Prefix   | Example           | Translation       |
|-------------|----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| I           | **ndi-** | Ndiyathanda        | I love            |
| You (sg)    | **u-**   | Uyathanda          | You love          |
| He/She      | **u-**   | Uthetha            | He/She speaks     |
| We          | **si-**  | Siyabathanda       | We love them      |
| You (pl)    | **ni-**  | Niyasibona         | You see us        |
| They        | **ba-**  | Bayafunda          | They learn        |

---

### 🧩 2.2 Object Infixes

These are inserted between the subject prefix and verb root to mark the object of the verb.

| Object      | Infix    | Example             | Meaning             |
|-------------|----------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Me          | **-ndi-**| Niyandibona         | You (pl) see me     |
| You (sg)    | **-ku-** | Singakufundisa      | We can teach you    |
| You (pl)    | **-ni-** | Sisanifundisa       | We still teach you  |
| Us          | **-si-** | Ndingasifundisa     | I can teach us      |
| Them        | **-ba-** | Ndiyabafundisa?     | Do I teach them?    |

---

### ⏳ 2.3 Tense and Modal Markers

These modify the verb to reflect time and possibility.

| Marker      | Function             | Example              | Translation               |
|-------------|----------------------|----------------------|----------------------------|
| **-ya-**     | Present tense marker | Ndiyathanda          | I love                     |
| **sa-**      | Still                | Ndisafunda           | I still learn              |
| **nga-**     | Can (ability)       | Ndinganibona         | Can I see you?             |
| **kwan-/kw-**| Also                | Ukwandibona?         | Do you also see me?        |
